Why Original Research Wins

ISEF/IRIS judges specifically look for original data + original methodology. You cannot just call an API. The sweet spot: fine-tune an open-source model on a niche local dataset that nobody has studied before.

6 High-Impact Research Fields for You — Specific to Assam/NE India
🌿 Agriculture · ISEF Category: Plant Biology or Engineering
AI-Powered Tea Leaf Disease Detection Using Transfer Learning
Assam produces 50% of India's tea (worth billions). Tea blight and grey blight destroy entire harvests. Fine-tune an EfficientNet-B4 on PlantVillage + 500 self-collected Assam tea photos. This makes it geographically unique — no prior ISEF project has done this.
Dataset: PlantVillage (Kaggle) + collect 300–500 photos yourself using your phone
Model: EfficientNet-B4 via fast.ai (Phase 2 skill)
ISEF Category: Plant Sciences or Computational Biology
Difficulty: Medium · Impact: Affects millions of tea farmers
🌊 Environment · ISEF Category: Environmental Engineering
Brahmaputra Flood Prediction Using LSTM Time Series
Assam is one of the most flood-affected states in the world. Historical river level + rainfall data is publicly available from India's CWC. Build an LSTM model that predicts flood risk 24–72 hours ahead. Massive social impact, highly specific regional dataset.
Dataset: CWC (Central Water Commission) historical data + IMD rainfall records — both free
Model: LSTM using PyTorch (Phase 2 skill)
ISEF Category: Environmental Engineering
Difficulty: Medium-Hard · Impact: Could actually save lives
🗣️ NLP / Linguistics · ISEF Category: Computer Science
Low-Resource NLP: Sentiment Analysis for Assamese Language
Assamese is spoken by 15+ million people but has almost no NLP research. Fine-tune XLM-RoBERTa (a multilingual transformer) on self-labeled Assamese social media text. This is a genuinely underexplored field — your research could be the first of its kind.
Dataset: Scrape 3,000–5,000 Assamese tweets/news sentences + label them yourself
Model: XLM-RoBERTa fine-tuned via Hugging Face (Phase 3 skill)
ISEF Category: Systems Software / Linguistics
Difficulty: Hard but high reward · Impact: Represents 15M+ people
🦏 Conservation · ISEF Category: Animal Sciences
Computer Vision for Rhino Population Monitoring in Kaziranga
Kaziranga has 2/3 of the world's one-horned rhinos. Contact the Kaziranga field office — they may share camera trap images. Train a YOLOv8 model for rhino detection and individual identification. Extraordinary conservation impact + unique dataset.
Dataset: Reach out to Kaziranga National Park / WWF India for camera trap footage
Model: YOLOv8 (easy to use, fast training on Colab free GPU)
ISEF Category: Animal Sciences

Hackathon Winning Formula
⚡ 24-Hour Hackathon Formula (Use This Every Time)
1

Hours 0–2: Ideation. Pick a problem in education, healthcare, or accessibility. Frame it: "Who is the user? What is the pain? How does AI solve it uniquely?" Sketch on paper.

2

Hours 2–5: MVP. Use Lovable.dev to generate your UI. Hook up OpenAI API or LangChain RAG. Get SOMETHING working with real data.

3

Hours 5–18: Polish. Real test cases, edge case handling, better UI. Add the AI feature that makes it genuinely useful — not just a chatbot wrapper.

4

Hours 18–22: Record a 2-minute demo video (essential). Build a 3-slide pitch: Problem → Solution → Impact metrics.

5

Hours 22–24: Submit + sleep. Don't add features. Polish your Devpost description. "Who will use this and why" should be crystal clear.

What Wins vs What Doesn't
✅ WINS
  • Specific real user + specific pain point clearly defined
  • AI feature that couldn't be done without AI
  • Live demo that actually works during presentation
  • Social impact narrative (education, health, accessibility)
  • Clean UI (Lovable builds this for you)
  • Team that can present confidently in 3 minutes
❌ LOSES
  • Generic AI chatbot ("ask me anything") with no focus
  • Demo that crashes or needs special setup to run
  • Overly technical presentation without clear "so what"
  • Too many features, none of them working well
  • No real user pain identified — "it could be useful for..."
  • Building a model from scratch during 24 hrs (use APIs)

From Project to Startup — The Gradual Path
Stage 1
Week 2–20: Build the project. Don't think "startup" yet. Just build something genuinely useful that solves a real problem. The Plant Disease Detector and AI Research Assistant both have startup DNA.
Stage 2
Week 20–28: Find 10 real users. Share your deployed project with 10 actual people who have the problem. Farmers, researchers, classmates. Ask "Would you pay $5/month for this?" If 3+ say yes — you have a product.
Stage 3
Week 28–36: Add a payment wall. Use Stripe (30 min to integrate). Start with a free tier (5 queries/month) + paid tier ($9/month for unlimited). Revenue validates the idea. Even $50/month is a signal.
Stage 4
After Week 36: Apply for programs. YC's Startup School (free, online), Antler India, India's NASSCOM 10K Startups. Having paying users and a live product = strong application. Winning hackathons with this product builds credibility.
Stage 5
Ongoing: Tell the story. Write about what you're building on LinkedIn. Founders who write about their journey get inbound interest from investors, users, and journalists. Your ISEF research + startup = extraordinary college application narrative.
